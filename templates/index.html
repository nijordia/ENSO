The ENSO phenomenon in the Eastern Pacific

ENSO's Economic Impact

Extreme weather events such as ENSO significantly impact global economies, particularly in regions like Peru. The 1997-98 El Niño caused widespread devastation, with heavy rains leading to floods and landslides, disrupting economic activities and increasing financial stress on microfinance institutions (MFIs). While early events saw MFIs struggling with loan defaults, improved risk management and client diversification strategies helped mitigate the impact of the 2015-16 event. These changes highlight the adaptive capacity of financial institutions in the face of climate variability.

ENSO Episode 1997-1998

The 1997-98 ENSO event was one of the strongest on record, bringing severe floods and landslides to Peru. It led to significant economic disruptions, affecting agriculture, infrastructure, and finance. Microfinance institutions struggled with high loan defaults as borrowers faced financial distress due to property damage and crop losses. Government intervention was necessary to stabilize the financial sector and aid recovery efforts.

ENSO Episode 2015-2016

Unlike the 1997-98 ENSO, the 2015-16 event, while still strong, had a relatively lower financial impact on Peruvian microfinance institutions. Over the years, MFIs diversified their portfolios and adopted better risk management strategies. Additionally, advancements in climate prediction allowed for early interventions, reducing overall economic damage. Despite ongoing challenges, the financial sector demonstrated greater resilience.
